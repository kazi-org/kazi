### E45 -- One system: roadmap-scope planning, plan-as-generated-view, escalation-as-data, skill retirement (P1, ADR-0056)

Before kazi the operator ran engineering work through a plan/apply pair of
orchestration skills; kazi grew alongside them and, ADR by ADR, absorbed their
load-bearing pieces (predicates, native scheduler, computed waves, Gherkin use
cases, doc lifecycle, and via E44 landing + process contract + gate providers +
preflight). What remains is TWO parallel systems maintaining copies of the same
facts -- `acc:` lines vs predicates, a hand-authored Waves section vs the
`needs`-DAG schedule, checkpoint files vs the read-model -- and ~1,800 lines of
untested orchestration prose outside any release process. ADR-0056 commits to ONE
system for engineering work: `kazi plan --project` authors a roadmap as a goal
DAG (`needs` edges BETWEEN goals -- ADR-0028 lifted one level); rolling-wave is
native (an outline phase is a goal whose predicate is "phase N's goal-set exists,
floor-passed, approved", so planning itself converges and a standing roadmap
apply triggers the next planning pass when the frontier lands); the plan document
becomes a generated VIEW (`kazi plan render`, output never input); discovery
folds into the plan verb; the ADR-0035 escalation ladder moves into the goal-file
as an `[escalation]` block (data, not core policy); the self-teaching artifacts
grow to carry the full surface; and the external skills retire ONLY after a
zero-skill idea->landed-PR->live-verify dogfood passes (the ADR-0031
gated-on-proof discipline). Non-engineering work is explicitly OUT (ADR-0056
decision 6).

Existing seams reused: the `needs`-DAG group scheduler + `--explain`
(ADR-0027/0028) lifted from groups-within-a-goal to goals-within-a-roadmap; the
proposal lifecycle + clarify floor + read-model projections (plan/approve/
list-proposed); `kazi init` stack detection + `init --discover` use-case
discovery (ADR-0054); the E44 `[integration]`/landing chain (T44.1-T44.3) that a
roadmap apply inherits per goal; the T30.3 tiering signals + `docs/
tiering-signals.md` (the `[escalation]` trigger vocabulary already documented);
`kazi install-skill`/`AGENTS.md` + the ADR-0052 coherence guard; and
`parse_plan.py`-era plan semantics as the rendering target for `plan render`.

**Waves and rolling-wave note.** Wave A (roadmap core, T45.1-T45.5) and Wave B
(discovery + escalation, T45.6-T45.7) are independent of E44 and of each other
except where deps say otherwise. Wave C (retirement, T45.8-T45.10) is the LATER
PHASE and is deliberately coarser-grained (rolling-wave, ADR-0056 decision 2
applied to this epic itself): T45.9 and T45.10 gate on both Wave A and the E44
landing dogfood (T44.14), and their decomposition may be refined by a planning
pass once Wave A lands -- do not front-load detail into them.
